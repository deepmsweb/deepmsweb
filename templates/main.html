Multiple Sclerosis

Multiple sclerosis (MS) is a neurological disease that affects the nervous system.

This disease causes inflammation by damaging myelin sheaths which surrounds and isolates nerve fibers in the central nervous system (CNS). MS affects the white and gray matter in the brain, spinal cord and optic nerve. In addition, MS is a chronic inflammatory, demyelinating and neurodegenerative CNS disease. Although the cause of MS is not known precisely, it is believed to involve a combination of genetic susceptibility, abnormalities in the immune system and environmental factors that combine to trigger disease.

MS is one of the most common causes of non-traumatic diseases among young and middle-aged adults. Healthcare costs directly related to MS are estimated to be more than 10 billion dollars in the USA annually. Moreover, it is known that more than 2 million people worldwide have MS.In a worldwide study, it is stated that the number of MS patients increased from 2.3 million in 2013 to 2.8 million in 2020. It is seen that the number of people with MS disease varies by country. The rate of MS case is between 110-140 per 100.000 people in the northern regions of the USA, between 57-78 people in the southern regions, between 115-140 people in Western Europe, and between 81-101 people in Australia . In addition, it has been put forth by the studies that approximately 20 thousand people die per year due to MS disease.

Follow-Up

Change detection in Multiple Sclerosis plaques is of great importance for physicians. For the comfort of the patients, the change of MS plaques in each MRI scan helps to determine the course of recovery or progression of the disease.
